Expert Breast Implant Removal in Scottsdale

Whether you're experiencing complications, have concerns about breast implant illness, or simply wish to return to your natural shape, Dr. Aldo Guerra provides compassionate, expert care for breast implant removal. With extensive experience in explant surgery, Dr. Aldo Guerra helps patients safely transition to life without implants.

Breast implant removal, also known as explant surgery, is becoming increasingly common as women's preferences and circumstances change over time. Dr. Aldo Guerra understands the personal nature of this decision and provides individualized care to help you achieve your desired outcome.

Reasons for Breast Implant Removal

Women choose to have their breast implants removed for various reasons:

  • Personal preference: Desire to return to a natural look or smaller breast size
  • Capsular contracture: Hardening of scar tissue around the implant causing discomfort or distortion
  • Implant rupture or leak: Silicone or saline implant failure
  • Breast implant illness (BII): Systemic symptoms some patients attribute to their implants
  • Implant age: Desire to remove older implants without replacement
  • Lifestyle changes: Changes in activity level, weight, or aesthetic preferences
  • Asymmetry or malposition: Implants that have shifted or no longer look natural

Surgical Options

Dr. Aldo Guerra offers several approaches to breast implant removal, tailored to your specific needs:

Simple Implant Removal

The implant is removed while leaving the surrounding capsule tissue in place. This is often appropriate when the capsule is thin and healthy, and the patient has no concerns about breast implant illness.

Capsulectomy

The implant and surrounding scar tissue capsule are removed separately. This is common for patients with capsular contracture or those who want more thorough removal of foreign material.

En Bloc Capsulectomy

The implant and entire capsule are removed together as one intact unit, without opening the capsule. This technique is preferred for:

  • Suspected or confirmed implant rupture
  • Patients concerned about breast implant illness
  • Those who want complete removal of all implant-related material

Implant Removal with Breast Lift

Many patients choose to combine implant removal with a breast lift (mastopexy) to address excess skin and restore a more youthful breast shape. This is particularly beneficial for patients who have had large implants or have experienced significant changes in skin elasticity.

The Consultation Process

During your consultation, Dr. Aldo Guerra will:

  • Review your medical history and reasons for seeking removal
  • Examine your breasts and assess your current implants
  • Discuss imaging studies if needed (ultrasound or MRI)
  • Explain your surgical options and recommend the best approach
  • Discuss whether a breast lift would benefit your results
  • Show you before and after photos of similar patients
  • Address any questions or concerns you may have

Recovery & Results

Recovery from breast implant removal is typically easier than the original augmentation surgery. Most patients experience:

  • Week 1: Rest at home, mild to moderate discomfort managed with medication
  • Week 2: Return to light activities and desk work
  • Week 3-4: Resume most normal activities
  • Week 6: Return to exercise and strenuous activities

Your breasts will appear smaller immediately after surgery. Final results develop over 3-6 months as swelling resolves and tissues settle. Many patients report feeling immediate relief, particularly those who experienced symptoms associated with breast implant illness.

What to Expect After Removal

The appearance of your breasts after implant removal depends on several factors:

  • Original breast size before augmentation
  • Size and type of implants removed
  • Duration implants were in place
  • Skin elasticity and quality
  • Whether a lift is performed

Dr. Aldo Guerra will provide realistic expectations during your consultation and help you understand what results you can achieve with or without additional procedures.

Insurance coverage varies. Removal may be covered if there are documented complications such as rupture, capsular contracture, or infection. Cosmetic removal without medical necessity is typically not covered. Our team can help you understand your coverage options and provide necessary documentation for insurance claims.

Not all patients need a breast lift after implant removal. The need for a lift depends on your skin elasticity, the size of your implants, and how long they were in place. During your consultation, Dr. Aldo Guerra will assess your situation and discuss whether a lift would improve your results.

Most patients recover more quickly from implant removal than from the original augmentation. You can typically return to light activities within 1-2 weeks and resume normal activities within 3-4 weeks. Full recovery, including return to exercise, usually takes about 6 weeks.
