Rhinoplasty (Nose Surgery)

Home / Face / Rhinoplasty

Expert Rhinoplasty in Scottsdale

Rhinoplasty, commonly known as a "nose job," is one of the most transformative facial procedures available. Dr. Aldo Guerra specializes in creating balanced, natural-looking results that harmonize with your unique facial features while maintaining your ethnic identity.

Whether you want to refine the shape of your nose, correct breathing problems, or address asymmetry from a previous injury, Dr. Aldo Guerra combines surgical expertise with an artistic eye to achieve results that look naturally beautiful.

Benefits of Rhinoplasty

  • Improve facial harmony and balance
  • Reduce or increase nose size
  • Refine the nasal tip (bulbous, drooping, or upturned)
  • Straighten a crooked nose
  • Smooth bumps or depressions on the bridge
  • Correct asymmetry
  • Improve breathing function
  • Repair injury-related deformities

Types of Rhinoplasty

Cosmetic Rhinoplasty

Focuses on improving the aesthetic appearance of the nose, including size, shape, and proportion relative to other facial features.

Functional Rhinoplasty

Addresses structural issues that affect breathing, such as a deviated septum or collapsed nasal valves, often combined with cosmetic improvements.

Revision Rhinoplasty

Corrects problems from a previous rhinoplasty procedure. This specialized surgery requires advanced expertise due to altered anatomy and scar tissue.

Ethnic Rhinoplasty

Refines the nose while preserving and honoring the patient's ethnic characteristics and cultural identity.

Open vs. Closed Rhinoplasty

Dr. Aldo Guerra uses two primary surgical approaches depending on your anatomy and goals:

  • Open Rhinoplasty: A small incision is made across the columella (the tissue between the nostrils), providing direct visibility for complex reshaping
  • Closed Rhinoplasty: All incisions are made inside the nose, leaving no visible external scars. Ideal for less extensive modifications

The Consultation Process

During your rhinoplasty consultation, Dr. Aldo Guerra will:

  • Discuss your aesthetic goals and concerns
  • Evaluate your nasal structure and facial proportions
  • Assess your breathing function
  • Review your medical history
  • Use computer imaging to visualize potential results
  • Recommend the best surgical approach for your goals

Recovery & Results

After rhinoplasty, you will wear a nasal splint for about one week. Swelling and bruising around the eyes is common but subsides within 2-3 weeks. Most patients return to work within 1-2 weeks.

While you'll see improvement once the splint is removed, final results develop gradually over 12-18 months as subtle swelling resolves and the nasal tissues settle into their new shape.
