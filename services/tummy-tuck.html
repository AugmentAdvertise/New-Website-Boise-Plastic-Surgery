Tummy Tuck (Abdominoplasty)

Home / Body / Tummy Tuck

Achieve a Flatter, More Toned Abdomen with Tummy Tuck Surgery in Scottsdale

A tummy tuck, or abdominoplasty, is a transformative body contouring procedure that removes excess skin and fat while tightening weakened abdominal muscles. Dr. Aldo Guerra specializes in customized abdominoplasty techniques that deliver dramatic yet natural-looking results tailored to your unique body and goals.

Whether you are looking to restore your pre-pregnancy figure, address changes from significant weight loss, or simply achieve the flat, toned stomach that diet and exercise alone cannot provide, Dr. Aldo Guerra will work closely with you to develop a personalized surgical plan that addresses your specific concerns.

Benefits of Tummy Tuck Surgery

  • Remove excess, loose skin from the abdomen
  • Eliminate stubborn fat deposits resistant to diet and exercise
  • Repair separated or weakened abdominal muscles (diastasis recti)
  • Create a flatter, more toned abdominal profile
  • Improve waistline definition and body proportions
  • Reduce the appearance of stretch marks (on removed skin)
  • Enhance comfort and fit in clothing
  • Boost self-confidence and body image

Types of Tummy Tuck Procedures

Dr. Aldo Guerra offers several abdominoplasty techniques to address varying degrees of concern. During your consultation, he will recommend the approach best suited to your anatomy and goals.

Mini Tummy Tuck

A mini abdominoplasty is ideal for patients with minimal excess skin and fat limited to the area below the navel. This less invasive procedure involves a shorter incision and typically does not require repositioning of the belly button. Recovery is faster, making it an excellent option for those with localized concerns who do not need extensive correction.

Full Tummy Tuck

The standard or full tummy tuck addresses the entire abdominal area, from the ribcage to the pubic region. This procedure involves a hip-to-hip incision along the lower abdomen, removal of excess skin and fat, tightening of the abdominal muscles, and repositioning of the belly button. It is the most commonly performed abdominoplasty and delivers comprehensive results.

Extended Tummy Tuck

An extended abdominoplasty is designed for patients who have excess skin that extends around the flanks and lower back, often following massive weight loss. The incision extends further around the hips to address these areas, providing more extensive body contouring. This technique creates a smoother, more proportionate silhouette from front to back.

Circumferential Tummy Tuck (Body Lift)

For patients with significant excess skin around the entire midsection, a circumferential abdominoplasty, also known as a lower body lift, addresses the abdomen, flanks, hips, and lower back in a single procedure. This comprehensive approach is often recommended for patients who have undergone massive weight loss.

Who Is a Good Candidate for Abdominoplasty?

Ideal candidates for a tummy tuck in Scottsdale include individuals who:

  • Have excess loose or sagging skin on the abdomen
  • Have weakened or separated abdominal muscles
  • Have stubborn fat deposits that do not respond to diet and exercise
  • Are at or near their stable goal weight
  • Are in good overall health without conditions that impair healing
  • Do not smoke or are willing to quit before and after surgery
  • Have realistic expectations about the outcome
  • Are not planning future pregnancies

The Tummy Tuck Procedure

Tummy tuck surgery is performed under general anesthesia at our accredited surgical facility. The specific steps depend on the type of abdominoplasty being performed:

  1. Incision: Dr. Aldo Guerra makes an incision along the lower abdomen, typically within the bikini line, where it can be concealed by underwear or swimwear.
  2. Muscle Repair: The underlying abdominal muscles are tightened and sutured together, correcting any separation (diastasis recti) and creating a firmer abdominal wall.
  3. Skin and Fat Removal: Excess skin and fat are removed, and the remaining skin is redraped over the newly contoured abdomen.
  4. Belly Button Repositioning: For full and extended tummy tucks, a new opening is created for the belly button, which is positioned naturally on the tightened abdomen.
  5. Closure: The incisions are closed with sutures, and drains may be placed to prevent fluid accumulation.

Recovery After Tummy Tuck Surgery

Recovery from abdominoplasty requires patience and proper care to achieve optimal results:

  • First Week: You will experience swelling, bruising, and discomfort managed with prescribed medications. Walking is encouraged to promote circulation.
  • Weeks 2-3: Most patients can return to light desk work. Drains are typically removed within 1-2 weeks.
  • Weeks 4-6: Swelling continues to decrease, and you can gradually resume more activities.
  • Weeks 8-12: Most patients can return to strenuous exercise and activities after clearance from Dr. Aldo Guerra.

You will wear a compression garment for several weeks to support healing and minimize swelling. Final results become visible as swelling fully resolves over 3-6 months.

Combining Procedures

A tummy tuck is often combined with other procedures to achieve comprehensive body contouring:

  • Liposuction: For enhanced fat removal and contouring of the flanks and waist
  • Mommy Makeover: Combining abdominoplasty with breast surgery for post-pregnancy rejuvenation
  • Thigh Lift: For patients seeking comprehensive lower body improvement
  • Brazilian Butt Lift (BBL): Using removed fat to enhance the buttocks
