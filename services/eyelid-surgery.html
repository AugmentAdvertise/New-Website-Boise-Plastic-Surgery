Rejuvenate Your Eyes with Blepharoplasty in Boise

Eyelid surgery, or blepharoplasty, is one of the most transformative facial procedures, addressing droopy eyelids, under-eye bags, and excess skin that makes you look tired or older than you feel. Dr. Jonathan Kramer's meticulous technique creates natural-looking results that brighten and rejuvenate your entire appearance.

The delicate skin around your eyes is often the first area to show signs of aging. Genetics, sun exposure, and the natural aging process can cause the upper eyelids to droop, puffiness to develop, and bags to form under the eyes. These changes can make you appear tired, sad, or angry even when you're well-rested and happy.

Benefits of Eyelid Surgery

  • Elimination of droopy, heavy upper eyelids
  • Removal of under-eye bags and puffiness
  • More alert, refreshed, and youthful appearance
  • Improved peripheral vision (for upper blepharoplasty)
  • Natural-looking results that don't look "done"
  • Long-lasting results (7-15+ years)
  • Quick recovery with minimal downtime

Types of Eyelid Surgery

Upper Blepharoplasty

Upper eyelid surgery removes excess skin and fat from the upper eyelids that creates a hooded appearance or impairs vision. The incision is hidden within the natural crease of the eyelid, making it virtually invisible once healed.

Lower Blepharoplasty

Lower eyelid surgery addresses puffiness, bags, and excess skin under the eyes. Depending on your needs, Dr. Jonathan Kramer may use a transconjunctival approach (incision inside the lower lid) or an external approach (incision just below the lash line).

Combined Upper and Lower Blepharoplasty

Many patients benefit from addressing both the upper and lower eyelids in a single procedure for comprehensive rejuvenation.

Am I a Candidate for Eyelid Surgery?

You may be an ideal candidate for eyelid surgery if you have:

  • Droopy or heavy upper eyelids
  • Excess skin obscuring the natural fold of the upper eyelid
  • Puffiness or bags under the eyes
  • Dark circles or hollowness under the eyes
  • Fine wrinkles and excess skin of the lower eyelid
  • Impaired peripheral vision due to excess eyelid skin

Ideal candidates are in good overall health, non-smokers, and have no serious eye conditions. Dr. Jonathan Kramer will evaluate your eyes during your consultation to determine the best approach.

The Consultation Process

Your eyelid surgery journey begins with a comprehensive consultation. During this appointment, you will:

  • Discuss your concerns and aesthetic goals
  • Review your medical history and any eye conditions
  • Undergo an examination of your eyelids and surrounding structures
  • Learn about the surgical technique recommended for you
  • View before and after photos of similar patients
  • Discuss whether additional procedures would benefit you

Recovery & Results

Eyelid surgery has one of the quickest recoveries of any facial procedure. After surgery, you may experience some swelling, bruising, and temporary blurred vision.

Recovery Timeline:

  • Days 1-3: Rest with head elevated, use cold compresses, apply prescribed ointment
  • Days 5-7: Sutures removed, bruising begins to fade
  • Week 2: Most bruising and swelling resolved, return to work
  • Week 3-4: Resume all normal activities including exercise

Results are visible almost immediately and continue to improve as swelling subsides. Final results are apparent within 1-3 months. Upper eyelid results typically last 7-15 years, while lower eyelid results can last even longer.
