Expert Breast Implant Revision Surgery in Boise

Breast implant revision is a specialized surgical procedure designed to replace, adjust, or correct existing breast implants. Whether you are experiencing a complication, want to update your look, or simply feel it is time for a change, Dr. Jonathan Kramer offers expert breast implant revision surgery tailored to your individual needs and goals.

As a board-certified plastic surgeon with extensive experience in breast surgery, Dr. Kramer understands that revision procedures require an even higher level of precision and expertise than primary augmentation. He takes the time to thoroughly evaluate each patient, develop a personalized surgical plan, and deliver results that restore confidence and satisfaction.

Reasons for Breast Implant Revision

There are many reasons why a patient may seek breast implant revision surgery. Dr. Kramer regularly helps patients address the following concerns:

Capsular Contracture

Capsular contracture occurs when the scar tissue (capsule) that naturally forms around the implant tightens and hardens, causing the breast to feel firm, look distorted, or become painful. Dr. Kramer can remove the thickened capsule (capsulectomy) and replace the implant, often changing the implant pocket to reduce the risk of recurrence.

Implant Rupture

Saline implants that rupture will deflate noticeably, while silicone implant ruptures may be silent and detected only through imaging. In either case, the ruptured implant should be removed and replaced. Dr. Kramer carefully removes all implant material and any leaked silicone gel before placing the new implant.

Size Change

Many women find that their aesthetic preferences evolve over time. Whether you want to go larger for a more noticeable enhancement or downsize for a more natural look, revision surgery allows you to update your implant size to match your current lifestyle and goals.

Implant Malposition

Implants can shift from their ideal position over time, resulting in issues such as bottoming out, lateral displacement, or symmastia (implants moving too close together). Dr. Kramer uses advanced techniques including internal suturing and pocket adjustment to reposition the implants and create a more natural, symmetrical result.

Rippling

Visible rippling or wrinkling of the implant can occur, especially with saline implants or in patients with thin breast tissue. Revision surgery can address this by switching to a different implant type, adding a supportive mesh, or changing the implant placement from above to below the chest muscle.

Lifestyle Changes

Significant weight changes, pregnancy, breastfeeding, or aging can alter the appearance of augmented breasts. Revision surgery can restore a youthful, balanced look by addressing changes in breast tissue, skin elasticity, and implant position that occur naturally over time.

The Breast Implant Revision Process

Dr. Jonathan Kramer takes a thorough, patient-centered approach to breast implant revision:

  • Comprehensive Evaluation: During your consultation, Dr. Kramer examines your current implants, reviews any imaging studies, discusses your concerns, and identifies the underlying issues to be addressed
  • Personalized Surgical Plan: Based on your evaluation and goals, Dr. Kramer develops a customized revision plan that may include implant exchange, capsulectomy, pocket modification, fat grafting, or a combination of techniques
  • Implant Selection: If you are receiving new implants, Dr. Kramer helps you choose the ideal size, shape, profile, and type (silicone gel, structured, or saline) to achieve your desired outcome
  • Surgical Technique: Dr. Kramer utilizes the most current surgical techniques, often using existing incision sites to minimize additional scarring. The procedure typically takes 1.5 to 3 hours under general anesthesia
  • Concurrent Procedures: Revision surgery is often combined with a breast lift to address sagging or stretched skin, providing a comprehensive rejuvenation of the breast

Recovery & Results

Recovery from breast implant revision varies depending on the complexity of the procedure. Here is a general timeline of what to expect:

  • First Week: Expect moderate swelling, bruising, and discomfort managed with prescribed medications. A supportive surgical bra should be worn at all times. Most patients can perform light daily activities within a few days
  • Weeks 2-3: Swelling continues to improve. Many patients return to desk work and driving within 1-2 weeks. Avoid lifting anything over 5 pounds
  • Weeks 4-6: Gradually resume more normal activities, including light exercise. Continue wearing a supportive bra as directed by Dr. Kramer
  • Months 2-6: Final results become apparent as implants settle into their optimal position and all swelling resolves. Scars continue to fade and mature over the following months

Dr. Kramer and his team provide detailed post-operative instructions and are available throughout your recovery to answer any questions and ensure the best possible outcome.
