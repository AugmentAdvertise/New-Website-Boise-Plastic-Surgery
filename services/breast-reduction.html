Find Relief and Confidence with Breast Reduction in Scottsdale

Breast reduction surgery offers life-changing relief for women experiencing physical discomfort and limitations due to overly large breasts. Dr. Aldo Guerra combines technical expertise with artistic precision to create smaller, lighter, and beautifully proportioned breasts that complement your body.

Large, heavy breasts can cause chronic back pain, neck pain, shoulder grooving from bra straps, skin irritation, and difficulty with physical activities. Beyond the physical symptoms, many women feel self-conscious about their breast size and struggle to find comfortable clothing. Breast reduction addresses both the functional and aesthetic concerns, helping you feel more comfortable in your body.

Benefits of Breast Reduction

  • Relief from chronic back, neck, and shoulder pain
  • Elimination of skin irritation and rashes under the breasts
  • Improved posture and physical comfort
  • Greater ease in exercising and physical activities
  • Better-fitting clothing and bras
  • Enhanced body proportions and silhouette
  • Increased self-confidence and quality of life

Am I a Candidate for Breast Reduction?

You may be an ideal candidate for breast reduction if you experience:

  • Chronic back, neck, or shoulder pain related to breast weight
  • Deep grooves in your shoulders from bra straps
  • Skin irritation or rashes beneath your breasts
  • Difficulty finding bras and clothing that fit properly
  • Limited ability to participate in physical activities
  • Self-consciousness about your breast size
  • Numbness or tingling in your arms or hands

Ideal candidates are in good overall health, non-smokers, and have realistic expectations about the results. Dr. Aldo Guerra will evaluate your individual situation during your consultation to determine if breast reduction is right for you.

Your breast reduction journey begins with a comprehensive consultation with Dr. Aldo Guerra. During this appointment, you will:

  • Review your medical history and current health
  • Undergo a physical examination of your breasts

Surgical Technique

Breast reduction surgery removes excess breast tissue, fat, and skin to achieve smaller, lighter, and more proportionate breasts. Dr. Aldo Guerra uses advanced techniques to minimize scarring while creating beautiful, natural results.

Incision Patterns

The incision pattern depends on your breast size, shape, and the amount of reduction needed:

  • Anchor (Inverted-T) Incision: The most common technique for significant reductions, with incisions around the areola, vertically down to the breast crease, and along the crease
  • Vertical (Lollipop) Incision: For moderate reductions, with incisions around the areola and vertically down to the breast crease
  • Liposuction-Only: For patients who need minimal reduction and have good skin elasticity, using only small incisions for liposuction

Dr. Aldo Guerra repositions the nipple and areola to a higher, more youthful position while maintaining sensation and the ability to breastfeed when possible.

Recovery & Results

After surgery, you'll wear a supportive surgical bra to minimize swelling and support healing. Most patients experience some discomfort, swelling, and bruising for the first 1-2 weeks.

Recovery Timeline:

  • Week 1-2: Rest at home, light activities only
  • Week 2-4: Return to desk work, continue wearing surgical bra
  • Week 4-6: Resume most normal activities
  • Week 6-8: Return to exercise and strenuous activities

Most patients report immediate relief from back and neck pain, even before the swelling fully subsides. Final results become apparent over 3-6 months as swelling resolves and the breasts settle into their new shape.

Insurance Coverage

Breast reduction may be covered by insurance when it is deemed medically necessary. Coverage typically requires documentation of symptoms such as chronic pain, skin problems, and interference with daily activities. Our office can help you navigate the insurance approval process and provide the necessary documentation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Breast reduction may be covered by insurance when it is deemed medically necessary to relieve symptoms such as chronic back pain, neck pain, or skin irritation. Requirements vary by insurance company, but typically include documentation of symptoms, failed conservative treatments, and a minimum amount of tissue to be removed. Our team can help you navigate the insurance approval process.

Most patients can return to light activities within 1-2 weeks and resume normal activities within 4-6 weeks. Strenuous exercise and heavy lifting should be avoided for 6-8 weeks. Many patients report feeling significantly better within the first few weeks as the weight and strain on their back and shoulders is relieved.

Many women can still breastfeed after breast reduction, though the ability may be reduced depending on the surgical technique used and the amount of tissue removed. Breast reduction does result in some scarring, but Dr. Aldo Guerra uses techniques to minimize their visibility. Scars typically fade significantly over 12-18 months. Most patients feel the relief from symptoms and improved breast shape far outweigh any concerns about scarring. Scars can usually be concealed by bras and swimwear.

The amount of reduction depends on your goals and anatomy. During your consultation, Dr. Aldo Guerra will discuss your desired outcome and recommend an appropriate reduction size.
