Enhance Your Facial Balance with Chin Surgery in Boise

Chin surgery, also known as mentoplasty, is a transformative facial procedure that enhances the chin to create improved facial balance and harmony. Dr. Jonathan Kramer specializes in chin augmentation and reshaping techniques that deliver natural-looking results, bringing your facial features into beautiful proportion.

Whether you want to add projection to a receding chin, reduce a chin that is too prominent, or correct asymmetry, Dr. Jonathan Kramer combines surgical expertise with an artistic eye to achieve results that complement your unique facial structure.

Benefits of Chin Surgery

  • Improved facial harmony and balance
  • Enhanced chin projection and definition
  • Correction of a weak or receding chin
  • Reduction of an overly prominent chin
  • More defined jawline and profile
  • Better proportion between chin and other facial features
  • Correction of chin asymmetry
  • Permanent, natural-looking results

Types of Chin Surgery

Chin Augmentation (Implants)

Chin implants are the gold standard for adding projection to a weak or receding chin. Made of solid silicone, they come in various sizes and shapes to customize your results. The implant is placed through a small incision either inside the mouth or beneath the chin.

Sliding Genioplasty

For patients who need more significant changes or vertical adjustments, sliding genioplasty involves cutting and repositioning the chin bone itself. This technique allows for precise control over chin position in all directions -- forward, backward, up, or down.

Chin Reduction

For patients with an overly prominent or wide chin, chin reduction surgery reshapes the bone to create a more balanced appearance. This procedure sculpts the chin to better fit the proportions of the face.

Chin Reshaping

Combining augmentation or reduction with contouring techniques, chin reshaping addresses multiple aspects of the chin including width, height, projection, and symmetry for comprehensive improvement.

Chin Surgery Approaches

Dr. Jonathan Kramer uses two primary surgical approaches depending on your anatomy and goals:

  • Intraoral Approach: An incision is made inside the mouth along the lower lip, leaving no visible external scars. Ideal for implant placement and some genioplasty procedures
  • Submental Approach: A small incision is made beneath the chin in a natural skin crease, providing direct access for implant placement or bone reshaping. The scar becomes virtually invisible once healed

The Consultation Process

During your chin surgery consultation, Dr. Jonathan Kramer will:

  • Discuss your aesthetic goals and concerns
  • Evaluate your chin structure and facial proportions
  • Analyze your profile and facial balance
  • Review your medical history
  • Use imaging to visualize potential results
  • Recommend the best surgical approach for your goals

Recovery & Results

After chin surgery, you will experience some swelling, bruising, and tightness in the chin and jaw area for the first 1-2 weeks. Most patients return to work within 7-10 days.

While you will see improvement once initial swelling subsides, final results develop gradually over 2-3 months as all swelling resolves and the chin tissues settle into their new shape. Chin implant results are permanent.
