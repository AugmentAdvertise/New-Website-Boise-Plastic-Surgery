Restore Your Pre-Pregnancy Body with a Mommy Makeover in Boise

Pregnancy and breastfeeding are incredible experiences, but they can leave lasting changes to your body that diet and exercise alone cannot address. A mommy makeover combines multiple procedures into a single surgical session to help you reclaim the body you love and restore your confidence.

Dr. Jonathan Kramer understands that every woman's post-pregnancy body is unique. That's why he takes a personalized approach to each mommy makeover, carefully selecting and combining procedures to address your specific concerns and help you achieve your aesthetic goals.

What is a Mommy Makeover?

A mommy makeover is not a single procedure but rather a customized combination of surgical treatments designed to address the physical changes that occur after pregnancy and breastfeeding. By combining multiple procedures into one surgery, you benefit from a single recovery period and achieve comprehensive, harmonious results.

Common Procedures Included in a Mommy Makeover

Breast Enhancement

Pregnancy and breastfeeding often lead to changes in breast volume, shape, and position. Depending on your needs, Dr. Jonathan Kramer may recommend:

  • Breast Augmentation: Restore lost volume and enhance breast fullness with implants
  • Breast Lift (Mastopexy): Elevate sagging breasts and reposition the nipples for a more youthful appearance
  • Breast Augmentation with Lift: Combine both procedures for comprehensive enhancement
  • Breast Reduction: Reduce overly large breasts that may have developed during pregnancy

Tummy Tuck (Abdominoplasty)

The tummy tuck is often the cornerstone of a mommy makeover. This procedure addresses:

  • Loose, excess abdominal skin that won't respond to exercise
  • Separated abdominal muscles (diastasis recti) caused by pregnancy
  • Stubborn pockets of fat around the midsection
  • Stretch marks on the lower abdomen (those removed with excess skin)

Liposuction

Liposuction is frequently added to refine body contours and remove stubborn fat deposits that resist diet and exercise. Common treatment areas include:

  • Flanks (love handles)
  • Hips and outer thighs
  • Inner thighs
  • Back and bra area
  • Upper arms

Additional Procedure Options

Depending on your goals, Dr. Jonathan Kramer may recommend adding:

  • Thigh Lift: Address sagging skin on the inner or outer thighs
  • Arm Lift (Brachioplasty): Remove excess skin and fat from the upper arms
  • Buttock Enhancement: Lift or augment the buttocks for improved contour
  • Labiaplasty: Address changes to the labia that may have occurred during childbirth

Benefits of Combining Procedures

  • Single recovery period instead of multiple separate recoveries
  • One anesthesia session reduces overall surgical risk
  • Coordinated results for a harmonious overall appearance
  • Cost savings compared to separate procedures
  • Less total time away from your family and daily responsibilities

Are You a Good Candidate for a Mommy Makeover?

The ideal candidate for a mommy makeover:

  • Has completed her family (not planning future pregnancies)
  • Is at or near her ideal, stable body weight
  • Has finished breastfeeding for at least 3-6 months
  • Is in good overall health without medical conditions that could impair healing
  • Does not smoke or is willing to quit before and after surgery
  • Has realistic expectations about the outcomes
  • Has adequate help at home during the recovery period

Recovery After Your Mommy Makeover

Recovery from a mommy makeover requires planning, especially for mothers with young children. Here's what to expect:

First 1-2 Weeks

You will need full-time help at home. Focus on rest, taking prescribed medications, and light walking to promote circulation. You should not lift anything heavier than 5-10 pounds, including your children.

Weeks 2-4

Most patients can return to light desk work after 2-3 weeks. You may gradually resume light activities but should still avoid heavy lifting and strenuous exercise.

Weeks 4-8

You can slowly return to normal activities. Most patients can resume exercise around 6-8 weeks, starting with low-impact activities and gradually increasing intensity.

3-6 Months

Final results become visible as swelling fully resolves. Scars continue to fade and mature over the following months.

Long-Lasting Results

With proper maintenance through a healthy diet and regular exercise, your mommy makeover results can last for many years. Significant weight fluctuations or future pregnancies may affect your results, which is why we recommend completing your family before undergoing this procedure.

Frequently Asked Questions

A mommy makeover is customized to each patient but typically includes a combination of breast enhancement (augmentation, lift, or both), tummy tuck (abdominoplasty), and liposuction. Additional procedures may include thigh lift, arm lift, or buttock enhancement depending on your goals.

Most patients take 2-3 weeks off work and can return to light activities within 2-4 weeks. Full recovery, including resuming strenuous exercise, typically takes 6-8 weeks. Having help at home, especially with young children, is essential during the first 1-2 weeks.

We recommend waiting at least 6-12 months after childbirth and 3-6 months after finishing breastfeeding. This allows your body to stabilize, your weight to normalize, and ensures optimal surgical results.

While it's safe to become pregnant after a mommy makeover, future pregnancies may affect your results. For this reason, we recommend completing your family before undergoing the procedure to maintain long-lasting results.
